ABOUT PS5 PRO

The PlayStation 5 (PS5) is a home video game console developed by Sony Interactive Entertainment, serving as the successor to the PlayStation 4 and part of the ninth generation of video game consoles, alongside Microsoft's Xbox Series X/S. It was announced in April 2019 and launched on November 12, 2020, in select regions including Australia, Japan, New Zealand, North America, and South Korea, with a worldwide release following on November 19, 2020. The console features a custom AMD system on a chip (SoC) with an 8-core AMD Zen 2 CPU operating at a variable frequency up to 3.5 GHz and an AMD RDNA 2 GPU with 36 compute units, delivering a peak performance of 10.28 TFLOPS. A PlayStation 5 Pro model was released on November 7, 2024, featuring a faster GPU, enhanced ray tracing capabilities, and AI-driven upscaling technology.

PROS:


Impressive Performance: Fast load times due to a high-speed custom SSD, with most games running at 60fps or even 120fps for very smooth gameplay.
Excellent Graphics: Advanced GPU supports 4K gaming, ray tracing, and realistic lighting. The PS5 Pro model further enhances these graphics with AI upscaling and better ray tracing.
DualSense Controller: Features haptic feedback and adaptive triggers, providing more immersive gaming experiences.
Exclusive Games: Strong lineup of exclusive titles like Demon's Souls, Returnal, Spider-Man, and more.
Backward Compatibility: Most PS4 games are playable on PS5, often with improved performance.

CONS:

Large Size: The console is quite big and may not fit easily in every entertainment setup.
Limited Storage (Original Model): The standard PS5 only offers 667GB-1TB of usable storage, which can fill up quickly.
Controller Size: Some users may find the DualSense controller too large.
Scarcity of Must-Have Exclusives: While there are some excellent games, a few reviewers wish for more must-play exclusives.
No Optical Drive or Stand (PS5 Pro): To use physical discs with the PS5 Pro, you need to purchase a separate drive; the stand is also sold separately.
